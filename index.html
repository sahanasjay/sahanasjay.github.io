Hi, I'm Sahana!

I'm a reporter who writes and codes, using R and Python to collect, analyze and parse data for investigative stories.

This fall, I'm interning on the data team at the Washington Post — one of four students participating in the Post’s first fall internship program.

Over the spring and summer, I worked as a data journalist for the Howard Center for Investigative Journalism's data team. There, I wrapped a project on OSHA regulations during the COVID-19 pandemic and launched one looking into white-owned newspapers’ role in supporting and inciting racial terror.

I’ve served as a staff writer, copy chief, special edition editor and deputy managing editor at The Diamondback, the University of Maryland’s student newspaper. I’ve written about city development, education, housing and crime, and penned features on community events.

I’m a senior at the university studying journalism and criminology & criminal justice. After I graduate this June, I’m looking for positions where I can analyze, visualize and write about data.

I’m at home working in R and Python. I've written a web scraper, handled APIs and JSON and done plenty of data wrangling and analysis. I’m proficient with HTML and CSS, and always looking to improve my JavaScript skills! I created this website myself.

When I'm not writing or coding, I'm trying to match my mom's cooking skills, reading or going on spontaneous weekend trips with my friends. I'm an avid reader and gardener, with a passion for tropical shrubbery and edible plants.
