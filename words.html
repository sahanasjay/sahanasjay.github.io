The Washington Post

  • Desperate, angry, destructive: How Americans morphed into a mob

    My analysis of suspects and charging data related to the January 6th Capitol Riot returned the key findings that drove this Pulitzer-prize winning story. My work revealed that the majority of people charged with assault didn't seem to be affiliated with far-right extremist groups.

  • D.C. traffic deaths at 14-year high with low-income areas hardest hit

    I collected, cleaned, joined and analyzed traffic fatalities data from multiple agencies. Using that data, I created choropleth maps and ran spatial joins in R to generate and visualize the findings that drove this story — findings that told us lower-income communities bore the geographic brunt of the deaths, and that most of the victims were Black. I spent time talking to residents of some of the most heavily impacted neighborhoods about their experiences, grounding the piece in their stories.

  • Buffalo’s East Side was a food desert. The shooting made things worse.

    I fact-checked and analyzed grocery store and pharmacy records for this story, generating the data underlying the map and finding that Buffalo's majority-White areas have 22 percent more pharmacies than its majority-Black areas.

  • Judges have declined U.S.-proposed sentences in two-thirds of Jan. 6 cases so far

    I wrote functions in R to pull the count of any given charge minus those dismissed by guilty pleas, contributing to this story.

  • Longer, more frequent outages afflict the U.S. power grid as states fail to prepare for climate change

    Using data from the U.S. Energy Information Administration, I calculated the average number of minutes of power outage experienced by a customer in a given state in 2020, as well as the national average. The results of my analysis are displayed in this story's map graphic.
